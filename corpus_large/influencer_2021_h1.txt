date: 2021-06-10
source: Nexara Influencer Channel Audit H1 FY2022

Mid-year audit reveals deteriorating returns on celebrity-tier influencer activity. Engagement rate across celebrity partnerships has declined to 0.7 percent from 0.9 percent in FY2020, consistent with platform-wide compression of reach for commercial content on large accounts.

Macro-influencer cohort (200K to 1M followers) has outperformed on cost-efficiency. CPE for macro tier: Rs 5.1. CPE for celebrity tier: Rs 22.4. The efficiency gap has prompted a mid-year reallocation.

New budget split from Q2 FY2022: celebrity 35 percent, macro 50 percent, micro pilot 15 percent. Conversion tracking via first-party data matching shows macro influencers driving 2.1x higher post-exposure order rate compared to celebrity tier, controlling for audience demographics.

MMM update: influencer channel contribution revised to 7.4 percent of incremental orders. Macro tier adstock half-life estimated at 13 days, longer than celebrity tier at 9 days, suggesting stronger memory effects.
